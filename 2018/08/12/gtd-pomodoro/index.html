GTD + Pomodoro: Managing Work As Resource Allocation Problems

Aug 12, 2018 5:22· 435 words · 3 minutes read

A great part of most organizational systems (like GTD) is focused on destilling next actions based on all input one gets. These actions should eventually reflect the highest ROI for one’s time. You start capturing, processing processing, organizing and prioritizing to get to this beautiful list of next actions. All fine and dandy. What now?

For the inherently lazy among us, I devised a simple practice for the these execution phases. Adhering to a single practice like this removes ambiguity and uncertainness, freeing up mental resources for precisely that which is the most valuable: hammering out those tasks.

1. The Daily Practice

The practice is quite simple.

  • Select the three Most Important Tasks (MITs)
  • Allocate the average daily pomodoro capacity (ADPC) to those three MITs
  • Start banging out those pomodoro’s

Select the three Most Important Tasks (MITs)

Nothing new here: open the todo todo list, and select three of the Most Important Tasks at this very moment in time. Three. Tops. Capping the amount of tasks on which you will concentrate on a given day brings in focus and prevent your days being frittered away by urgent-but-not-that-important tasks. Be strict on yourself: do not allow other tasks before these three MITs are out of the way.

Allocate the average daily pomodoro capacity (ADPC) to those three MITs

By knowing the amount of pomdoro’s one is able to complete on a given day, it becomes possible to start managing time as a resource allocation problem. Pomodoro’s are scarce goods: you are able to push through a given amount of them each day. Knowing this, each of these pomodoro’s should be allocated to the MITs that have the highest ROI. Logging pomodoro’s comes in very handy for this.

Pomodoro log

After having a sense of your ADPC, planning a workday can look something like this.

  • Monday, 13th of August
    • MIT #1: 🍅🍅🍅
    • MIT #2: 🍅🍅
    • MIT #3: 🍅🍅🍅🍅
    • ADPC: 🍅🍅🍅🍅🍅🍅🍅🍅🍅

Start banging out those pomodoro’s

This is where the magic happens. Adhere to the following golden rules:

Pomodoro: Golden Rules**

  • Each pomodoro has a chrystal-clear defined activity which can be completed in 25 minutes. This is the key aspect of working with pomodoro’s: between start and end of each pomodoro no other activity is allowed. Defend this with your life.

  • Each pomodoro has to be completed: when disrupted, add the lost time afterwards

  • Motivation = (expectancy * value )/(impulsivess * delay)
